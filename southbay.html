What Exactly is El Niño & ENSO?

El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO) is a periodic climate phenomenon driven by the interaction between the Pacific Ocean and the atmosphere. Under normal conditions, strong easterly trade winds blow surface water toward Asia, causing nutrient-rich cold water to upwell along the California and South American coasts.

During an El Niño event, these trade winds collapse or even reverse. Warm surface water sloshes eastward toward the Americas, capping oceanic upwelling and dramatically altering global atmospheric circulation. For Southern California, this massive pool of equatorial heat frequently acts as an atmospheric steering wheel, pulling the North Pacific Jet Stream southward and aiming a conveyor belt of moisture directly at our coastline.

🏖️ How El Niño Specifically Impacts the South Bay

🌧️

Atmospheric Rivers & Flooding

A supercharged jet stream increases the frequency of subtropical atmospheric rivers slamming into Hermosa, Redondo, and Manhattan Beach. Expect localized urban flooding along low-lying sections of PCH, Topaz Street, and Hermosa Valley during high-intensity storm bursts.

🌊

High Surf & Coastal Erosion

Strong winter storms generate relentless W and WSW swells. When paired with El Niño-driven sea level rises (thermal expansion) and astronomical King Tides, severe beach erosion occurs. Manhattan Beach Pier and Topaz Jetty frequently face overwash and sand berm depletion.

🎣

Warm-Water Marine Life Shifts

Local water temperatures can surge 3°F–6°F above normal, sometimes hovering near 70°F well into autumn. This brings exotic pelagic sportfish like Yellowtail, Mahi-Mahi, and Bluefin Tuna unusually close to King Harbor, along with swarms of Pelagic Red Crabs washing ashore.

⛰️

Palos Verdes Bluff Instability

Back-to-back winter deluges deeply saturate coastal soils and clay layers across the Palos Verdes Peninsula. This hydrological loading significantly accelerates landslide activity, bluff retreat, and road deformation around Portuguese Bend, Palos Verdes Estates, and Torrance Beach cliffs.

🛡️ South Bay Resident Checklist

When an El Niño Advisory is active, South Bay homeowners and boaters should prepare in advance for extreme winter weather cycles:

  • ✔ Check Drainage & Gutters: Clear roof gutters and street storm drains before heavy Pacific atmospheric rivers arrive.
  • ✔ Locate City Sandbag Stations: Redondo, Hermosa, and Manhattan Beach public works yards distribute free sandbags to residents during storm warnings.
  • ✔ Inspect Harbor Moorings: Boaters in King Harbor should check dock lines and bilge pumps ahead of high WSW swell events.
  • ✔ Monitor PWS Telemetry: Keep an eye on our live Barometer trend; rapid pressure drops below 1010 hPa signal approaching storm fronts.
